Tim Berners-Lee, in full Sir Tim Berners-Lee, (born June 8, 1955, London, England), British computer scientist, generally credited as the inventor of the World Wide Web. Berners-Lee is the director of the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C), which oversees the continued development of the Web. He co-founded (with his then-wife-to-be Rosemary Leith) the World Wide Web Foundation.
Tim Berners-Lee was born on the 8th of June, 1955 in London, United Kingdom. His parents, Conway Berners-Lee and Mary Lee Woods were computer scientists as well as British mathematicians. He is a family man married to Rosemary Leith since 2014. Before Leith, he was married to Nancy Carlson and divorced in 2011. He is a father of two, Ben Berners-Lee and Alice Berners-Lee.
